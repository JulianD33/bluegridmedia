Is LSA Worth It for Handyman Businesses?

The short answer is yes, but with a clear-eyed view of the math. Handyman has a lower average ticket than most trades. You're not doing $3,000 AC replacements or $8,000 roofing jobs. A typical handyman visit runs $150 to $400, which means the economics look different than they do for a plumber or electrician.

Here's what makes it work. First, lead costs in handyman are genuinely low. You're looking at $20 to $45 per lead in most markets, which is some of the cheapest real-estate on the entire LSA platform. Second, handyman has something no other trade can match: repeat customers who call back. A homeowner who gets a great ceiling fan installation calls you back for the leaky faucet. Then the fence repair. Then the TV mount for the bedroom. That first $30 lead you paid for can generate $500 to $750 in business over 12 months if you show up on time and do clean work.

The businesses where LSA doesn't work for handyman are typically the ones with a service radius that's too large (too much windshield time between jobs), too few job types enabled (missing entire categories of search traffic), or response times that are too slow (homeowners in this category call 2 to 3 contractors). Nail the fundamentals and LSA can fill your calendar steadily year-round.

What LSA Looks Like for Handyman Businesses

Above organic listings. Your business name, star rating, number of reviews, and a click-to-call button are right there, and the homeowner can contact you without even clicking through to a website.

For handyman businesses specifically, two things matter a lot. One is the Google Verified badge. Handyman is a category with an unusually high number of informal operators, Craigslist handymen, and unlicensed individuals. Homeowners know this. The Google background check and insurance verification that your listing carries tells them you are a legitimate business, not someone who might disappear mid-job. That badge does real marketing work in this category.

The second thing is job type visibility. Handyman is one of the few LSA categories where Google prominently displays the specific job types you offer directly on your listing. That's a huge deal. When someone searches "garbage disposal installation near me" and your ad specifically shows that service, the conversion rate on that click is much higher than if they just see "handyman" and have to guess whether you do that job. Enable every service you legitimately offer and Google surfaces you more precisely.

Licensing for Handyman: The State-by-State Complication

This section matters a lot for handyman businesses specifically, because no other trade has licensing rules this inconsistent across states. Electricians, plumbers, and HVAC technicians all face similar licensing requirements nationwide. Handyman is genuinely different in almost every state.

The most common scenario is the handyman exemption: you can work without a general contractor license as long as individual jobs stay under a dollar threshold. The threshold varies by state. Some states set it at $500. Others set it at $1,000. Florida draws the line at $1,000 per job. Texas has no statewide handyman license requirement at all, though individual cities may have requirements. California requires a contractor license for any job over $500, which is one of the strictest cutoffs in the country. Some states, like Wyoming and Nebraska, have specific handyman license designations that are separate from a general contractor license.

Why does this matter for LSA? Because Google will verify whatever your state legally requires. If your state requires a contractor license for jobs over $500 and you're operating on a contractor's license, that's what you submit. If your state has no handyman licensing requirement at all, you submit proof of insurance and a background check. If you're near the dollar threshold in a state like California, you need to know exactly where the line is before you start running jobs and before you submit your LSA application.

If you are near a dollar threshold, consider whether your business model fits under or over that line. Running jobs at $600 in California without a license is a legal and LSA eligibility risk. Know your state before you apply.

How to Set Up LSA for Your Handyman Business

The setup process is straightforward once you know what to expect. Here's what it looks like for a handyman business specifically:

  1. Start at ads.google.com/local-services-ads and create your profile. Add your business name, address (or service-area-based if you don't have a storefront), phone number, and business hours. Be accurate. Inconsistencies between your LSA profile and your Google Business Profile create verification delays.
  2. Select your service category. Choose "Handyman" as your primary category. From there, Google will show you the job type menu, which is where you'll spend the most time.
  3. Enable every job type you can legitimately deliver. This is the most important step in handyman LSA and is covered in detail in the visual below. Do not skip job types because you haven't done them lately. If it's a service you offer, enable it.
  4. Set your service area. Keep this tight. A 15 to 20 mile radius from your home base is ideal for most handyman businesses. You can always expand later, but starting small keeps jobs clustered and your schedule efficient.
  5. Upload your documentation. This varies by state, but typically includes: general liability insurance certificate, any required state contractor or handyman license, and business registration if applicable. Have clean digital copies ready to upload. Blurry photos of documents cause delays.
  6. Complete background checks. This is required for all owners and sometimes for technicians too. It's the slowest step in the verification process, so start it first. Plan for 1 to 2 weeks for this to clear.
  7. Connect your Google Business Profile. Your GBP reviews feed directly into your LSA listing. If you don't have a GBP or it has fewer than 10 reviews, build that up before or alongside your LSA application. Reviews matter immediately for your ranking.
  8. Add real photos. Photos of you on jobs, your vehicle, your tools, completed work. No stock photos. Google rewards complete profiles and customers are more likely to call when they can see a real person and real work.
  9. Set your budget. Start with Maximize Leads bidding and a weekly budget that targets roughly 8 to 12 leads per week at your expected cost per lead. For handyman, that's typically $250 to $450 per week to start. Adjust based on real data after the first 30 days.

What Does LSA Cost for Handyman?

Handyman is one of the more affordable LSA categories for cost per lead. In most U.S. markets you're looking at $20 to $45 per lead. Competitive metros like Los Angeles or Miami might push toward the higher end. Smaller markets can come in at $15 to $25 per lead. For a detailed breakdown of CPL benchmarks across all trades, see our guide to Google LSA pricing by industry.

Here's how the math works out in a typical scenario. Say your average CPL is $30. Your team answers 80% of calls (solid but realistic). Of the calls you answer, you book 70%. That gives you a cost per booked job of roughly $54. Against an average job value of $250, you're spending about 22% of revenue on lead acquisition, which is completely workable for a service business.

But the real math is the repeat business picture. In handyman, 25 to 35% of first-time customers become repeat customers within 12 months. If a customer calls you back 2 to 3 more times that year at an average of $200 per visit, your lifetime value on that original $30 lead climbs to $500 to $750. That's where handyman LSA economics become genuinely excellent. The lead cost looks ordinary on paper. The lifetime value makes it outstanding.

Where the economics break down is when you're running too wide a service area (driving 45 minutes between $150 jobs burns your profit fast), not following up on leads quickly enough (in handyman, the first contractor to respond often gets the booking), or not capturing repeat business intentionally. More on all of that below.

Scheduling Efficiency: The Handyman Profit Driver

Here's something that separates handyman economics from every other trade on LSA: volume per day. A plumber might do one or two jobs a day. A roofer does one job over several days. A handyman who is running efficiently does 2 to 4 jobs per day, sometimes more for small tasks. That changes how you should think about LSA entirely.

With a tight service area of 15 to 20 miles, you can stack jobs geographically. Morning jobs near the north end of your area, afternoon jobs near the south. Minimal drive time, maximum billable hours. That's where a $250 average job stops looking small and starts looking sustainable: three of those in a day is $750, five days a week is over $15,000 per month in revenue before materials.

LSA feeds that model specifically. When a lead comes in for a ceiling fan installation at 9am, your goal isn't just to book that job. It's to book it within a cluster you're already running near, so you're not making a 35-minute drive for a $175 job that leaves you stuck in traffic for the next one.

One strategy that works well in handyman LSA is keeping one afternoon slot open every day for same-day calls. Homeowners who call in the morning and want someone today are high-converting and high-urgency. They've already decided they're going to hire someone. The question is who shows up. If you can say "I can be there between 1 and 3" while your competitor says "earliest I've got is Thursday," you get the booking almost every time. Same-day availability is a competitive edge in this category.

Reviews for Handyman: Volume Over Specialization

Review strategy for handyman is a little different than for other trades, and it's worth understanding why. A plumber's reviews all basically say "fixed my pipes, showed up on time, recommended." An HVAC tech's reviews all say "AC working great, professional." The review text is fairly uniform because the job type is fairly uniform.

Handyman reviews can and should mention specific tasks. When a customer leaves a review saying "came out and fixed our leaky faucet, hung 3 TVs, and patched a spot in the drywall all in the same visit," that's actually valuable to your LSA ranking because Google reads review text for job type signals. A review that mentions "garbage disposal installation" reinforces that job type on your profile. A review that mentions "ceiling fan" and "drywall" reinforces those categories.

This means your review request strategy should be slightly more specific than in other trades. After a multi-task visit, you can say something like: "If you do leave a review, feel free to mention the specific things we worked on today, it helps other homeowners find us when they need the same kind of help." That's not gaming the system, it's just giving customers useful context for what to write.

On volume: aim for at least one new review per week when starting out. Handyman has more customer touchpoints per week than almost any other trade, which means you have more opportunities to ask. Build a simple system: text message review request within 2 hours of leaving the job. Short, direct link to your Google Business Profile review form. Don't wait until the end of the week to send a batch. Fresh requests convert better than day-old ones. For a full review strategy framework, see our LSA review strategy guide.

How to Rank Higher in Handyman LSA

LSA ranking works on several signals that Google weighs together. For handyman businesses specifically, a few of these matter more than in other categories:

  • Response time is your single biggest lever. In handyman, homeowners expect fast response. They're not scheduling a furnace tune-up three weeks out. They want someone who answers calls and books quickly. Google tracks your response time and factors it into your ranking. Answer every call within 15 minutes of it coming in, and respond to any message leads within 30 minutes. Businesses that answer immediately rank measurably better than those who call back hours later.
  • Job type completeness dramatically expands your reach. Each enabled job type opens your profile to an entirely separate category of searches. A profile with 6 job types competes for 6 search categories. A profile with 18 job types competes for 18. This is the fastest and most underutilized ranking lever in handyman LSA. Spend 20 minutes going through the full job type list and enable everything you can deliver.
  • Review velocity matters year-round. Because handyman isn't seasonal the way HVAC or roofing is, Google doesn't see natural review spikes and drops. That's good. It means consistent weekly reviews (2 to 4 per week once your operation is running) signal steady business activity. Don't let your review count plateau. Businesses that plateau at 40 or 50 reviews while competitors keep adding fall in ranking over time.
  • Budget adequacy during busy periods. Handyman demand picks up in spring and around major holidays when homeowners are hosting or doing home prep. If your weekly budget runs out mid-week during these periods, you stop showing up for searches. Build a slightly higher budget for March through May and again in September and October.
  • Profile photo completeness. Google rewards profiles with real photos. For handyman businesses, photos are especially persuasive because homeowners want to see who is coming into their home. A photo of your face, your work vehicle, and a few finished projects goes a long way both in Google's algorithm and in the homeowner's decision to call you instead of the blank profile next to you.

Common Mistakes Handyman Businesses Make With LSA

  • Service area too large. It feels like more coverage equals more leads, but in handyman it's the opposite. A 40-mile radius means you're driving 35 to 50 minutes between $175 jobs. That's a profitability killer. Keep the radius tight, let Google bring you jobs that cluster, and you'll make more money on fewer miles.
  • Too few job types enabled. This is the single most common missed opportunity in handyman LSA. Most businesses enable 5 to 8 job types when they could legitimately enable 15 to 20. Every unchecked box is an entire category of searches you simply don't appear for. Go through the full list and enable everything you can deliver.
  • Not stocking for listed job types. If you're going to list garbage disposal installation as a service, carry a couple of units in the van. If you're listing ceiling fan installation, have a standard assortment of mounting hardware. Showing up to a booked job without the materials needed and having to reschedule destroys your reputation in a category that runs almost entirely on reviews and repeat business.
  • Not asking for reviews after every single visit. Handyman has the highest customer-contact frequency of any trade. You might see 3 to 5 customers per day. If you're asking for reviews after each visit, you should be adding 15 to 25 reviews per week at steady state. If you're getting 2 to 3 per week, you're leaving most of your review opportunities on the table. Build the habit of texting a review link before you drive away from every job.
  • Quoting over the phone instead of booking and quoting on-site. Handyman jobs are notoriously difficult to quote accurately over the phone because they often expand once you're actually looking at the problem. More importantly, a homeowner who gets a phone quote and doesn't hear back from you for a day often calls the next contractor. Book a time, show up, see the job, quote on-site. Your close rate from on-site quotes will significantly outperform phone quotes.
  • Ignoring lead disputes. Handyman generates a higher volume of invalid leads than most trades because the category attracts searches from homeowners who are looking for licensed contractors (electricians, plumbers) but phrase their search in a way that matches handyman. Review your leads weekly and dispute any that are clearly out of scope, wrong number, or non-service requests. Credits from disputes can meaningfully reduce your effective cost per lead.

FAQ

Does a handyman need a license for Google LSA?

It depends entirely on your state. Many states have a handyman exemption that lets you work without a general contractor license as long as jobs stay under a dollar threshold, typically $500 to $1,000 per job. Some states require a license for any dollar amount. A handful of states have specific handyman license designations. Google will verify whatever your state legally requires, so look up your state's contractor licensing board rules specifically for handyman before you apply. Upload whatever documentation your state mandates and the process will go smoothly.

What job types should a handyman enable on LSA?

Every one you can genuinely deliver. Common options include furniture assembly, TV wall mounting, ceiling fan installation, light fixture installation, door installation and repair, window screen repair, caulking and weatherstripping, drywall repair, tile repair, faucet repair and installation, toilet repair and installation, garbage disposal installation, gutter cleaning, gutter repair, deck repair, fence repair, baby-proofing and safety installation, smart home device setup, and minor plumbing repairs. Most handyman businesses enable 5 to 8 types. Businesses that enable 15 to 20 see dramatically wider search coverage and significantly more leads per week.

How much does LSA cost for handyman businesses?

Handyman LSA leads typically run $20 to $45 per lead, which is lower than most other trades because average job tickets are smaller. The economics work well when you run multiple jobs per day in a tight service area with fast response times. Where the math really shines is repeat customers. A homeowner who calls you once often calls back 2 to 3 more times that year. A first-time lead that costs $30 can generate $500 to $750 in lifetime revenue if you do good work and follow up.

What service radius should a handyman set in LSA?

Keep it tight. A 15 to 20 mile radius from your base is ideal for most handyman businesses. Unlike a plumber or roofer who does one big job per day, a handyman can run 2 to 4 jobs daily. A wide service area means long drives between small-ticket jobs, which destroys your hourly profitability. A tight radius keeps jobs clustered, drive times short, and your daily job count high. That's where handyman income actually gets built.

How can handyman businesses get more repeat customers from LSA?

Three habits work well. First, leave every job with a simple printed card or magnet listing your most popular services so the homeowner thinks of you when the next thing needs fixing. Second, ask directly at the end of the visit if there is anything else on their list you can help with today or schedule for next week. Third, send a review request via text within 2 hours of leaving. Customers who leave a review are significantly more likely to call again. Handyman has more customer touchpoints per year than almost any other trade, so small follow-up habits compound into a steady repeat-business pipeline.

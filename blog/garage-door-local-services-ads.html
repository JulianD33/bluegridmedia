What Are Google Local Services Ads?

Google Local Services Ads (LSAs) are pay-per-lead ads that appear at the very top of Google search results — above traditional Google Ads and above organic listings. When someone searches "garage door repair near me" or "garage door spring replacement," LSA listings are the first thing they see.

Unlike traditional pay-per-click ads where you pay every time someone clicks your ad, with LSAs you only pay when a potential customer contacts you directly through the ad — by phone call or message. That distinction matters a lot for your budget.

Each LSA listing displays your business name, star rating, number of reviews, years in business, and the Google Guaranteed badge. That badge is the key differentiator. It signals to homeowners that Google has verified your business, checked your licenses, and confirmed your insurance.

How LSAs Differ From Other Google Advertising

  • Pay per lead, not per click — you're charged only when a customer contacts you
  • Google Guaranteed badge — builds instant trust with homeowners
  • Top-of-page placement — above all other ads and organic results
  • No keyword management required — Google matches your ad to relevant searches automatically
  • Lead dispute system — you can get credits for spam or invalid leads

Why LSAs Work Especially Well for Garage Door Companies

Garage door repair is one of the strongest verticals for Local Services Ads. Here's why the economics work in your favor.

When a homeowner's garage door spring breaks at 7am and their car is stuck inside, they're not browsing — they're calling the first trusted result they see. LSAs capture that moment. The search intent is immediate, the need is real, and the customer is ready to book. That's the ideal scenario for a pay-per-lead model.

Repeat and Referral Potential

A homeowner who has a great experience with your company will call you again for maintenance, upgrades, and new installs. They'll also refer neighbors. The lifetime value of a customer acquired through LSA is significantly higher than the initial job value.

Less Competition Than You'd Expect

Despite how effective LSAs are, many garage door companies in mid-size markets are still not running them — or are running them poorly. That creates an opportunity for businesses willing to set up their profile correctly and manage their account actively.

How To Set Up Local Services Ads for Your Garage Door Business

Getting started with LSAs requires completing Google's verification process. Here's a step-by-step breakdown.

Step 1: Create Your LSA Profile

Go to ads.google.com/local-services-ads and select your business category. For garage door companies, you'll select "Garage Door" under the home services category. Fill out your business name, address, service area, and contact information.

Step 2: Complete the Google Guaranteed Verification

This is the most important step and the one that takes the most time. Google will require:

  • A background check for business owners and employees who enter customer homes
  • Proof of general liability insurance (minimum coverage requirements vary by state)
  • Business license verification where applicable

Have your insurance certificate and license documents ready before you start. Delays in submitting documents are the most common reason the process takes longer than expected. Approval typically takes 2 to 6 weeks.

Step 3: Set Your Service Area and Job Types

Define the zip codes or cities you serve. Be specific — if you don't want leads from 45 minutes away, don't include those areas. Select the job types you offer: spring repair, opener installation, new door installation, cable repair, panel replacement, and so on. Only select job types you actually want leads for.

Step 4: Set Your Weekly Budget

Google uses a weekly budget model. Start with a budget that allows for at least 10 to 15 leads per week in your market. Going too low limits your visibility and makes it harder for the algorithm to optimize your account. You can adjust your budget at any time.

Step 5: Set Your Business Hours and Availability

Your LSA profile shows when you're available to take calls. If you offer 24/7 emergency service, set your hours accordingly. If you only take calls Monday through Saturday, set that. Leads that come in outside your listed hours are typically not charged.

Step 6: Collect Your First Reviews

Before your ads go live, reach out to recent customers and ask for Google reviews. Your review count and rating are visible on your LSA listing and directly affect how often your ad is shown. Aim for at least 10 reviews before launch.

How To Rank Higher in Garage Door LSA Results

Google doesn't show every LSA advertiser equally. The platform uses a ranking algorithm to decide which businesses appear at the top. Understanding that algorithm is how you get more leads without increasing your budget.

The Core LSA Ranking Factors

  • Review score and volume — businesses with more high-quality reviews rank higher
  • Responsiveness — how quickly and consistently you answer calls and respond to messages
  • Proximity — how close your business is to the searcher's location
  • Business hours — being available when customers search increases your ranking
  • Budget adequacy — accounts that regularly hit their budget cap may lose ranking
  • Dispute rate — disputing too many valid leads can negatively affect your account

Practical Steps To Improve Your Ranking

The single highest-leverage action you can take is building a consistent stream of new Google reviews. A business with 200 reviews at 4.8 stars will almost always outrank a competitor with 30 reviews at 4.9 stars. Volume matters as much as rating.

Answer every call. Google tracks your call answer rate. If you're missing calls during business hours, your ranking will suffer. If you can't answer personally, use a call answering service during peak hours.

Keep your profile complete and accurate. Make sure your service area, job types, and business hours are up to date. Incomplete profiles rank lower.

How Reviews and the Google Guaranteed Badge Affect Your Results

The Google Guaranteed badge does two things: it helps you rank, and it converts more searchers into callers. Homeowners are more likely to call a business with the badge than one without it, even if the non-badged business has a lower price.

Building Reviews That Move the Needle

The most effective review-building system is simple: ask every customer immediately after the job is complete. A technician who finishes a spring replacement and says "If everything looks good, would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It really helps our small business" will generate far more reviews than a follow-up email sent three days later.

Use a short, direct link to your Google review page. Send it via text immediately after the job. Make it as easy as possible for the customer to complete.

Responding to Reviews

Respond to every review — positive and negative. For positive reviews, a brief, genuine thank-you is enough. For negative reviews, respond professionally, acknowledge the concern, and offer to make it right. How you handle a negative review is often more persuasive to potential customers than the negative review itself.

Common LSA Mistakes Garage Door Companies Make

Most garage door companies that struggle with LSAs are making one or more of these mistakes.

  • Setting the budget too low. A $200/month budget in a competitive market will generate almost no leads. Google needs adequate budget to show your ads consistently.
  • Not answering calls. Missed calls hurt your ranking and waste your lead budget. Every unanswered call is a paid lead that went nowhere.
  • Ignoring the review process. Businesses that don't actively ask for reviews fall behind competitors who do. Reviews compound over time — start building them on day one.
  • Disputing valid leads. Some businesses dispute every lead they don't immediately book. Google monitors dispute patterns. Only dispute leads that are genuinely invalid: spam, wrong number, or outside your service area.
  • Selecting too many job types. If you select job types you don't actually want or can't service well, you'll receive leads you can't convert. Be selective.
  • Setting overly restrictive hours. If your hours are set to 9am–5pm Monday–Friday, you're missing the majority of emergency calls that happen evenings and weekends.
  • Not tracking lead quality. Know which leads converted to booked jobs and which didn't. That data helps you optimize your budget and service area over time.

How To Increase Booked Jobs From Your LSA Leads

Getting leads is only half the equation. Converting those leads into booked jobs is where the real money is made. Here's how to maximize your conversion rate.

Answer Every Call Within the First Two Rings

Speed is everything in garage door service. A homeowner with a broken spring is calling multiple companies. The first one to answer and offer a same-day appointment wins the job. If you can't answer personally, use a live answering service — not voicemail.

Give a Price Range on the First Call

Customers who ask "how much does it cost?" and get a vague answer will hang up and call the next company. Train your team to give honest price ranges based on the most common scenarios. "Spring replacement is typically between $150 and $250 depending on the spring type — we can give you an exact quote when we're on-site" is far more effective than "it depends."

Offer Same-Day or Next-Day Appointments

Garage door problems are urgent. If your earliest available appointment is three days out, you'll lose the lead. Build your scheduling to accommodate same-day service for emergency calls whenever possible.

Follow Up on Missed Calls Immediately

If you miss a call, call back within five minutes. After 30 minutes, the probability of booking that lead drops dramatically. Set up an automated text that fires immediately when a call is missed: "Hi, this is [Company Name]. We just missed your call — we're on another job but will call you back within 10 minutes. Is that okay?"

Track Your Lead-to-Job Conversion Rate

Know your numbers. If you're receiving 40 leads per month and booking 15 jobs, your conversion rate is 37.5%. That's a reasonable baseline. If your rate is below 25%, the problem is likely in your answering process, pricing communication, or scheduling availability — not the leads themselves.

Is Your LSA Budget Right for Your Market?

One of the most common questions we hear from garage door business owners is whether they're spending the right amount. The answer depends on your market, your capacity, and your growth goals.

A good rule of thumb: your LSA budget should be set to generate more leads than you can comfortably handle. If you're booking every lead you receive, you're probably underspending. If you're receiving leads you can't service, scale back your service area before cutting your budget.

Getting Started: Your First 30 Days on LSA

Here's a practical action plan for garage door companies launching LSAs for the first time.

Week 1: Setup and Verification

  • Create your LSA profile at ads.google.com/local-services-ads
  • Submit your insurance certificate and license documents immediately
  • Define your service area — start tighter than you think you need to
  • Select only the job types you actively want leads for

Week 2: Reviews and Profile Optimization

  • Contact your 20 most recent satisfied customers and ask for Google reviews
  • Set up a review request text template for future jobs
  • Complete every section of your LSA profile — photos, business description, service details
  • Set your business hours to reflect your actual availability, including emergency hours

Week 3: Launch and Monitor

  • Set your initial weekly budget — start at a level that allows 10+ leads per week
  • Ensure your team knows how to handle LSA calls and the importance of answering quickly
  • Set up call tracking if possible to monitor lead quality
  • Check your LSA dashboard daily for the first two weeks

Week 4: Optimize

  • Review your lead quality — dispute any genuinely invalid leads
  • Check your answer rate in the dashboard — aim for above 90%
  • Assess your conversion rate — how many leads became booked jobs?
  • Adjust your budget up or down based on lead volume and quality

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do Google Local Services Ads cost for garage door companies?

Garage door LSA leads typically cost between $20 and $75 per lead depending on your market, competition level, and the type of job. Emergency calls and spring replacements tend to cost more than routine maintenance leads.

Do garage door companies need to be Google Guaranteed to run LSAs?

Yes. To run Local Services Ads, garage door businesses must pass Google's background check and verification process, which results in the Google Guaranteed badge appearing on your ad.

How long does it take to get approved for garage door LSAs?

The approval process typically takes 2 to 6 weeks. It includes a background check, license verification, and insurance verification. Having your documents ready speeds up the process significantly.

How many leads can a garage door company expect from LSAs?

Volume depends on your market size and weekly budget. A garage door company in a mid-size market spending $500 to $1,000 per week can typically expect 15 to 40 leads per month.

Can I dispute bad leads from Google LSA?

Yes. Google allows you to dispute leads that are spam, wrong number, or outside your service area. Approved disputes result in a credit back to your account. Disputing invalid leads is an important part of managing your LSA budget.

Are LSAs better than Google Ads for garage door companies?

For most garage door companies, LSAs deliver a lower cost per lead and higher intent traffic than traditional Google Ads. However, Google Ads offer more control over targeting and messaging. Many companies run both simultaneously for maximum coverage.

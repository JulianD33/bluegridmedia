Is Google LSA Worth It for Pressure Washing Companies?

Yes, and the numbers are hard to argue with. Pressure washing has one of the best CPL-to-revenue ratios on the entire LSA platform precisely because the lead cost is low and the average ticket, especially with upsells, grows quickly.

Here is the basic math: at a $25 CPL and a 60% close rate, your cost per booked job is roughly $42. A standard house wash runs $175 to $300. That is already a four to seven times return on the lead cost before you have said a word about the driveway. Add a driveway cleaning for $100 to $150, a deck wash for $100 to $175, and a fence cleaning for $75 to $125, and that same call that started as a $200 house wash becomes a $550 to $800 whole-property package. Your cost per booked job stayed at 2. The ticket tripled.

Volume and job-stacking are the real play here. Unlike roofing or HVAC where one job might take a full day and close at 30 to 35%, pressure washing jobs are completed in one to three hours each. A single crew can run four to six jobs on a good day. When each job averages $300 to $400 with standard upsells, you are looking at $1,200 to $2,400 in daily revenue from a crew that spent maybe $150 to $250 in lead costs to fill the schedule. Setting Up Google LSA for Your Pressure Washing Business

Pressure washing is one of the easier LSA categories to get verified in. Most states do not require a specific pressure washing license, which removes a major verification bottleneck that other trades deal with. Your biggest requirements are general liability insurance and a registered business entity.

1
Create your LSA profile at ads.google.com Navigate to Local Services Ads and enter your business name, service areas, hours, and a business description. Write it plainly: "Professional pressure washing and soft washing serving the greater Charlotte area. House washing, driveway and concrete cleaning, deck and fence washing, roof soft washing, and commercial exterior cleaning. Fully insured, background-checked, and serving residential and commercial clients since 2019." That kind of direct, specific language outperforms vague agency-style copy every single time.

If you do roof soft washing but never checked that box, you do not show up for "roof cleaning near me" searches no matter how many reviews you have. Go through the full job type list in the next section and enable everything you are equipped to handle.

Google will verify the certificate of insurance during the approval process.
4
Submit background checks Google requires background checks for business owners and field employees. Start this early because it is the slowest step in the process, often taking one to two weeks. Do not wait until everything else is ready before submitting.

Connect your Google Business Profile Your GBP reviews feed directly into your LSA listing. Verify your GBP is accurate: correct hours, updated service area, current phone number, and active photo uploads. A neglected GBP drags down an otherwise strong LSA profile. A driveway going from jet black to bright white is a compelling image that does real selling work before the customer even calls. Upload 10 to 15 before/after photos at minimum. The Job-Stacking Strategy That Makes Pressure Washing Profitable

Pressure washing is a volume game in a way that most other home service trades are not. A plumber might do two to three jobs on a great day. An HVAC tech might do three to four. A pressure washing crew can realistically complete five to six residential jobs in a single day, each one taking one to three hours depending on scope. When each job averages $300, that is $1,500 in daily revenue from a single crew.

The math gets even better when you stack jobs geographically. Every minute your crew spends driving between jobs is time they are not generating revenue. The goal is to cluster three to five jobs in the same neighborhood or zip code on the same day. This is where LSA zip code targeting becomes genuinely valuable. When you serve specific zip codes, you can book jobs in proximity to each other and cut your drive time significantly.

How to build geographic clusters with LSA

When a new LSA lead comes in from a specific neighborhood, before you confirm the booking, check whether there are any other pending inquiries or upcoming jobs in that same area. If you can schedule two or three jobs within a few blocks of each other on the same day, you have essentially eliminated the dead time between them. Some pressure washing companies do this systematically by offering a small neighbor discount: "We are already going to be in your neighborhood on Thursday. If you can book the same day as your neighbor, I can do 0 off." It sounds simple, but it works.

The neighbor knock

While your crew is on-site cleaning a driveway or house, send one team member to knock on two or three neighboring doors. Keep it short: "Hi, we are cleaning your neighbor's driveway next door. If you have been thinking about getting yours done, we can fit you in this week while we are already in the area. We offer a neighbor discount since we are already here with the equipment." A 20% conversion rate on those door knocks adds one or two jobs to the schedule per day with zero advertising cost.

Building recurring annual relationships

Every completed job is a recurring revenue opportunity if you ask for it. At the end of every job, say something like: "We like to reach out to our customers every spring before the season picks up. Would it be alright if I added you to our annual reminder list? We will send you a note in February or March and you can book before the schedule fills up." Most homeowners say yes. You now have a customer worth 00 per year, every year, with no advertising cost after the first acquisition. Over five years, that $30 LSA lead becomes $1,500 in total customer revenue.

The Upsell Conversation That Doubles Your Ticket

Here is what most pressure washing companies get wrong on phone calls: they answer the question they were asked and nothing more. Customer says "how much to clean my driveway?" Company says "$120, want to book?" That is a $120 job.

Here is how a 50 job happens instead. Customer says "how much to clean my driveway?" Company says: "For the driveway we are usually around $120 to $150 depending on size. While I have you, is the driveway the main thing, or is the house siding or deck something you have been thinking about too? A lot of our customers find it makes sense to do the whole exterior at once since we are already there with the equipment. I can put together a full-property quote at the same time if that is helpful." Then stop talking and let them respond.

This is not pushy. It is asking a practical question that a lot of homeowners have not considered. Many of them have been meaning to get the whole property cleaned but only called because the driveway was the most visible problem. You just made it easy for them to say yes to everything at once.

Package pricing that works

The most effective upsell structure is a tiered package, not a la carte pricing. When customers see individual prices for every surface, they start mentally adding and subtracting to hit a budget. When they see a package, they are comparing packages, not line items.

"Most of our customers end up going with the Curb Appeal Package because it covers the house and driveway together, which is the most noticeable difference, and it is usually only $120 to $150 more than just the driveway. The real estate angle

Real estate agents are an underused recurring client for pressure washing. When a property is about to go on the market, sellers and their agents are actively looking for exterior cleaning services to boost curb appeal before listing photos. A $450 whole-property clean before listing photos is a $450 decision that can add $5,000 to $15,000 to a sale price. Build a referral relationship with two or three real estate agents in your market and you will have a reliable source of pre-listing jobs, especially in spring selling season, that do not require a single LSA dollar.

The race-to-the-bottom pricing trap. Pressure washing attracts more lowballers than almost any other exterior service. A guy with a pressure washer from Home Depot and a pickup truck can undercut your price by 40% on a driveway clean and make a thin margin on volume. Do not try to compete with that. What that operator does not have is a Google Verified badge, 50 reviews, before/after photos, professional equipment for soft washing, and the ability to do a whole-property clean in one visit. Homeowners calling from LSA are choosing trust signals over price far more often than you think. Keep your pricing at a level that sustains a real business, and let your reviews and photos do the selling. Cutting prices to compete with lowballers just trains your market to expect lowball pricing and erodes the margins that make the equipment and insurance worthwhile.

Seasonal Demand and When to Crank Up Your Budget

Pressure washing is one of the more seasonal trades on LSA, but the pattern is predictable enough that you can plan your budget around it rather than reacting to it.

Spring: the big surge (March through May)

This is your peak season. Homeowners emerge from winter, see the algae on the siding and the oil stains on the driveway that have been accumulating since October, and decide this is the year they are actually going to call someone. Search volume for pressure washing services spikes 200 to 400% compared to winter months depending on your region. Start increasing your LSA budget in late February so you are positioned before the peak, not catching up to it. The contractors who crank up spend in March capture the early-season demand when CPLs are still at their lowest before competition heats up.

Spring is also pre-listing season for real estate. Properties going on the market in April and May need exterior cleaning in March and April. If you have built relationships with real estate agents, your schedule fills up at the exact moment demand is highest.

Summer: steady demand with commercial opportunity (June through August)

Residential demand stays strong through summer, particularly for decks, patios, and pools being used actively. This is also the best time to develop commercial accounts. Property managers, restaurants with outdoor seating, retail centers, and office parks all want their exteriors maintained during the high-traffic summer months. A commercial contract at $300 to $500 per monthly visit is recurring revenue that does not require an LSA call each time.

Fall: the second wind (September through October)

Homeowners do another round of exterior prep before winter. Decks and patios get a final clean before the season ends. In some markets, this is also prime roof soft washing season as summer algae and moss growth becomes visible. Keep your budget active through October. Fall CPLs are often lower than spring because fewer competitors are spending heavily, and homeowners who search in fall tend to be decisive bookers since they know the weather window is closing.

Winter: reduce but do not stop (November through February)

In northern states, exterior cleaning demand drops significantly. Scale your budget back 50 to 70% from peak levels, but do not pause. Pausing LSA kills your ranking history and forces you to rebuild visibility from scratch when spring arrives. The ranking momentum you built by staying active through winter is worth the modest budget you will spend on a slower season. You will still get calls for commercial cleaning, interior concrete (garage floors, warehouse floors), and from homeowners in milder periods who notice the exterior needs attention.

Warm-climate markets: If you operate in Florida, Texas, Arizona, Southern California, or the Gulf Coast, pressure washing demand is active year-round. Algae and mildew growth in humid climates means houses need cleaning two to three times per year, not once. In these markets, you do not reduce your winter budget significantly. Q4 and Q1 can actually have lower CPLs because competition softens while demand stays strong.

How to Rank Higher in Pressure Washing LSA

Pressure washing ranking follows the same core algorithm as other LSA categories. For a complete breakdown of all seven ranking signals, see our Google LSA ranking factors guide. Here are the factors that matter most specifically for exterior cleaning.

Reviews are the single biggest lever

Pressure washing has a distinct advantage in review collection: the results are immediately visible and dramatic. A homeowner whose driveway went from oil-stained black to clean white is genuinely impressed. That is a high-emotion moment that translates into review action if you ask for it at the right time. Ask before you leave the job site, while the customer is still looking at the transformation. "If you are happy with how it came out, a quick Google review would really help us out. It only takes a minute." That is the highest-conversion moment you will ever have for review collection. For a full review system, see our LSA review strategy guide.

Before/after photos: your biggest ranking and conversion asset

This is where pressure washing has a visual advantage that most other trades do not. The before/after contrast in exterior cleaning is dramatic and compelling in a way that a plumbing repair or an electrical panel photo simply is not. A driveway going from charcoal to white, a house going from green-gray algae to bright clean siding, a deck going from weathered brown to looking almost new. These images do real persuasion work in your LSA listing before the customer even decides to call.

Take before/after photos on every single job. Use the same angle for before and after so the comparison is striking. Upload new photos regularly, at least two to three per week during your peak season. Google favors active profiles, and new photos signal an active business.

Response time: answer every call within minutes

Google tracks how quickly you answer calls and how often you miss them. Response rate and response speed both factor into your ranking. For pressure washing specifically, missed calls during spring peak season are painful: the customer calls the next verified company immediately. Set up call forwarding if you are on the job site, or use a virtual receptionist service for overflow. A missed call in April costs you a booked job and erodes your ranking simultaneously.

Job type completeness

Every unchecked job type is a search category you are invisible for. Enable everything you can deliver: house washing, driveway, deck, fence, roof, patio, commercial, graffiti removal, gutter exterior. Competitors who only have three or four job types enabled miss the searches for the rest. Job type breadth gives you coverage across a wider range of search queries with no additional per-lead cost.

Service area discipline

One of the most common ranking mistakes pressure washers make is setting a service area that is too large. A 40-mile radius sounds like more opportunity, but it dilutes your geographic relevance signal and sends your crew on long drives between jobs. Tighten your service area to the zones you can realistically serve efficiently. You rank better in a focused area than you rank weakly across a large one, and your job-stacking economics improve dramatically when jobs cluster in proximity.

Common LSA Mistakes Pressure Washing Companies Make

Only enabling 2 or 3 job types

The most common and most costly mistake. If you do roof soft washing but did not enable it, you are invisible for "roof cleaning near me." Same for deck washing, fence washing, commercial cleaning, and every other surface type. Go through the full job type list and enable everything you are equipped to handle. Every unchecked box is a category of searches where a competitor shows up and you do not.

Not doing the upsell conversation on every call

If you answer every call, quote the one thing they asked about, and take the booking without offering anything else, you are leaving significant revenue behind. The whole-property upsell conversation takes 60 seconds and adds $200 to $400 to the average ticket. Build it into your booking script so every call gets the full menu of options, not just the one the homeowner thought to ask about.

Slow response during spring peak

March through May is when the calls come in fast and customer patience for voicemail is lowest. During peak season, a missed call often means the customer called the next person on the list and booked before you called back. If you are on the job site all day and cannot answer the phone, get someone who can. Call forwarding, a part-time receptionist, or a virtual answering service is money well spent during the eight weeks of spring peak when your LSA is most valuable.

No follow-up system for annual recurring customers

Every customer you complete a job for is a potential 00 per year recurring revenue source. If you do not have a system to reach out to past customers in early spring with a reminder and a booking offer, you are re-acquiring them via LSA the following year at another $25 to $45 per lead. A simple annual follow-up text or email campaign to your customer list pays for itself immediately and compounds over time as your base of returning annual customers grows.

Quoting too low over the phone without seeing the property

Pressure washing jobs vary significantly in actual scope. A house wash can take 45 minutes on a small ranch-style home or three hours on a large two-story with extensive algae. Quoting a flat price over the phone without clarifying square footage, home size, or surface conditions sets you up for underpriced jobs and customer friction when the quote changes on-site. Qualify the job before quoting: "What size is the house approximately, and is the siding vinyl, brick, or stucco?" Two questions that protect your margin on every booking.

Service area set too large

A 40-mile service radius hurts your LSA ranking and your job economics simultaneously. Google weighs geographic proximity heavily for local search results. A wide radius dilutes your relevance signal and fills your schedule with jobs that require long drives between sites. Set your service area to the zones you can serve efficiently and stack jobs within.

Should Pressure Washing Companies Run LSA and Google Ads Together?

LSA is the right starting point for most pressure washing companies, and for many operators it is the only paid channel they need. The CPL is low, the lead quality is high, and the return on a well-run LSA campaign is strong. But there are scenarios where adding Google Ads makes sense.

LSA captures the ready-to-book customer. "Pressure washing near me," "driveway cleaning," "house wash service," "soft wash near me." These are people who have already decided to hire someone and are choosing between verified providers right now. LSA owns this moment completely.

Google Search Ads capture the planning-phase homeowner who LSA does not reach. Homeowners searching "how much does pressure washing cost," "how often should you pressure wash your house," "best way to clean vinyl siding," and "pressure washing vs soft washing" are not ready to call yet. They are doing research. A well-built landing page targeting these keywords builds email lists and nurtures future customers that LSA will never see. For a detailed comparison of both channels, see our Google Ads vs. LSA guide for contractors.

Commercial account development is another use case for Google Ads. If you are targeting property management companies, shopping centers, restaurants, or HOAs for recurring commercial contracts, Search Ads targeting "commercial pressure washing company" or "parking lot cleaning service" in your metro can generate commercial leads that are larger and stickier than individual residential calls.

Start with LSA alone. It is simpler to manage, the economics are excellent for pressure washing specifically, and it builds your review base quickly. Once your schedule is consistently full from LSA and you want to scale further, layer in Google Ads to capture commercial leads and planning-phase homeowners. Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between pressure washing and soft washing on LSA?
Both fall under the same LSA service categories. When setting up your profile, enable both "pressure washing" and "soft washing" job types if you offer both. Soft washing is the safer method for roofs, painted surfaces, and vinyl siding. If you only offer one, specify it in your profile description so customers understand what they are getting and you are not booking jobs outside your equipment capability.
Is pressure washing seasonal enough to make LSA worth it year-round?
Depends on your market. In warm-climate states like Florida, Texas, Arizona, and Southern California, pressure washing demand is nearly year-round. In northern states, spring (March through May) and fall (September through October) are peak, and winter is slow. Reduce your LSA budget in the off-season rather than pausing entirely. Keeping the account active protects your ranking and means you are positioned before spring demand spikes rather than rebuilding from scratch when it does.
How many jobs per month can I expect from LSA?
At a $1,000 to $1,500 per month budget, most pressure washing companies see 20 to 40 leads with a 50 to 60% close rate, resulting in 12 to 24 booked jobs per month. That is $4,000 to $9,000+ in revenue depending on your average ticket. Higher budgets during spring peak can deliver 50+ leads. For more context on what to expect, see our LSA lead volume benchmarks guide.
Do I need a license to run LSA as a pressure washing company?
Most states do not require a specific pressure washing license. You will need proof of general liability insurance (usually $500K to $1M minimum) and a registered business. Some states require a business license or contractor registration depending on the work scope. Google will specify exactly what documentation they need during the verification process. For a full walkthrough, see our Google Verified step-by-step guide.
Should I charge extra for roof cleaning on LSA leads?
Roof soft washing is a premium service that justifies premium pricing. At $300 to $600 for an average-size roof versus $150 to $250 for a house wash, it is one of the highest-margin jobs in the category. Enable it as a separate job type so you show up specifically for "roof cleaning near me" searches. There is also notably less competition for this specific job type than for house or driveway washing, which means lower CPL for a higher-ticket lead.
How do I compete with lowball pressure washers on LSA?
Compete on trust signals, not price. A lowball competitor without reviews, photos, or a Google Verified badge consistently loses to a verified business with 40+ reviews and dramatic before/after photos, even at a higher price point. Homeowners calling from LSA are choosing based on credibility, not just cost. For more on how LSA vets lead quality, that guide covers what makes a qualified LSA inquiry.

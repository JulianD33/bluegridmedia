Setting Up Google LSA for Your Gutter Business

Gutter company verification is relatively straightforward in most states. Most states treat gutter installation as a home improvement trade requiring a general contractor license or home improvement contractor registration. Cleaning is typically unlicensed but requires insurance. Because you're working at height, Google will scrutinize your general liability and workers compensation coverage closely. Have both ready before you start the application.

1
Create your LSA profile Go to ads.google.com and navigate to Local Services Ads. Enter your business name, service areas (start with your core markets, not 50 zip codes), your hours, and a business description that sounds like a gutter contractor wrote it. Something like: "Seamless gutter installation, gutter cleaning, gutter guard systems, and repair serving the greater Nashville area. We offer same-day cleaning during fall season and back every installation with a 5-year workmanship warranty." That specificity beats vague marketing copy every time.
2
Enable every job type you offer Most gutter contractors enable cleaning and installation, then stop. That leaves gutter guard installation, downspout repair, fascia repair, and commercial gutter services unchecked. Each unchecked box is a set of search queries you won't appear for. Go through the complete list in the next section and check everything you can deliver.
3
Submit background checks Required for business owners and field employees. Start this first because it typically takes one to two weeks. Don't wait until everything else is submitted. Get background checks in motion on day one of your application.
4
Upload your license and insurance documentation General liability insurance is non-negotiable, and for a trade working at heights, Google verifies it closely. Workers compensation is required if you have employees and will be checked in states that mandate it. Your contractor license or home improvement registration needs to be current. 5
Connect your Google Business Profile Your GBP reviews feed directly into your LSA listing. A gutter company with 45 reviews at 4.9 stars shows that right on the ad. Make sure your GBP is current: accurate service area, correct hours (especially your storm-response availability during fall), and recent before-and-after photos. A stale GBP drags down your LSA performance regardless of how strong your actual reviews are.
6
Set your budget and go live Start with Maximize Leads bid mode and a weekly budget targeting 8 to 12 leads per week. In most gutter markets, that's 00 to 00 per week outside peak season. The Gutter Seasonal Playbook

Gutter demand is more seasonal than almost any other home service trade. Understanding the two distinct peaks and two slow periods lets you budget intelligently instead of spending the same amount year-round and wondering why Q3 results look so different from Q4.

Fall (September through November): your biggest window

This is the peak. Leaves fall, gutters clog, the first heavy rain hits, and homeowners notice water pouring over the sides of the house for the first time all year. This is when cleaning demand is highest, when storm damage shows up, and when homeowners who have been putting off replacing their old gutters finally pull the trigger because they can see the problem with their own eyes.

Double your LSA budget in September and keep it elevated through November. In northern markets, this three-month window accounts for 40 to 50% of annual gutter cleaning volume. CPLs may be slightly higher due to competition, but the lead quality is excellent and close rates are strong because buyers are motivated by visible problems and the weather window closing before winter.

Spring (March through May): the post-winter inspection window

The second peak. Ice and snow damage shows up in the spring thaw: gutters that pulled loose over winter, downspouts that cracked, sections that sagged under ice dam weight. Homeowners also start spring cleaning, which includes gutters. Spring is when installation demand picks up as exterior renovation projects get underway and homeowners plan summer home improvements.

Increase your budget 30 to 40% above off-season baseline starting in early March. Spring leads often convert to larger jobs because winter damage is visible and the homeowner was already thinking about a full replacement.

Summer (June through August): lower volume, lower competition

Search volume drops significantly in most markets. Gutters are clean from spring, there's no leaf fall, and homeowners aren't thinking about gutters unless they're prepping a house for sale or just had storm damage. Reduce your LSA budget to 40 to 50% of peak levels but keep it running. Summer leads are cheaper because fewer gutter contractors are competing actively, and the homeowners who do search in summer are often high-intent (installation or full replacement) rather than casual inquiries.

Winter (December through February): maintenance mode

In northern markets, installation work nearly stops. Cleaning is minimal. Focus on commercial accounts (property managers need year-round service regardless of season) and keep a minimum budget running to maintain your ranking position. Do not pause entirely. Pausing LSA kills your ranking history and you'll spend weeks rebuilding your visibility when spring demand returns. Reduce budget, don't eliminate it.

Warm-climate adjustment: In markets like Florida, Texas, Arizona, Southern California, and similar regions, the seasonal pattern is different. Leaf fall is lighter, outdoor work continues through winter, and hurricane season (June through November) creates its own storm-surge demand. Adjust your budget peaks to your regional weather patterns, not to a generic northern template.

The Storm Surge Strategy

A major storm is the best lead-generation event a gutter company can have. After a significant wind, hail, or rain event, demand for gutter repair and cleaning spikes 40 to 80% in the affected area. Homeowners who never thought about their gutters suddenly have a very visible, very urgent problem. Sections are hanging off the house. Water is pooling near the foundation. The problem cannot be ignored until spring.

The challenge: every other gutter company in your market is getting those same calls. The ones who answer first, offer the fastest service, and close on the first call get the work. The ones who let calls go to voicemail or say "I can get there in four days" do not.

Before storm season: set yourself up to move fast

Set your LSA budget to "flexible" or increase your weekly cap before fall storm season begins. If your budget cap is too low, Google stops showing your ad mid-week once you hit the limit. During a storm surge, that's exactly when demand is highest and your budget should be working hardest. Check your budget settings in late August and make sure the cap isn't limiting your reach during your most profitable weeks.

During the surge: answer every call

Storm-damage leads are the highest-converting calls in home services. A homeowner looking at a section of gutter hanging off their house is not comparison shopping. They need someone who can come out immediately. Answer every call within the first few rings. If you're on a job, forward calls to someone who can schedule. A missed LSA call during a storm surge is a ,400 job that went to your competitor, plus a negative ranking signal for your account.

The storm-response close script

Have a script ready for storm calls. It goes something like: acknowledge the urgency ("Sounds like you got hit hard, we've been getting calls all morning"), confirm your availability ("We can get someone out today before 5 or first thing tomorrow, which works better for you?"), and close on a time before the call ends. Do not say "let me check the schedule and call you back." By the time you call back, that homeowner has already booked someone else.

The Gutter Guard Upsell That Doubles Your Ticket

Every cleaning job is a pitch opportunity, and most gutter contractors are leaving this money on the table completely. You're on the ladder, the gutters are packed with debris, and the homeowner has come outside to watch or chat. This is the perfect moment for a five-minute conversation that can add ,500 to ,000 to the ticket.

The pitch is simple

When gutters are heavily clogged or this is clearly not the first time cleaning has been needed, open with: "How often do you have these cleaned?" They'll say once or twice a year. You say: "We can keep doing that, it's 50 each time. Or we can install [product name] gutter guards today and you'll never have to think about this again. It runs about ,400 for your home size, and you'd break even in about five years." Then stop talking.

Gutter guard systems typically run ,500 to ,000 installed depending on the product line, home size, and linear footage. Micro-mesh systems run higher. Solid cover systems sit in the middle. The customer's calculation is simple: pay 50 twice a year forever, or pay once and be done. A real percentage of homeowners say yes when you make that math clear in plain language.

The "while I'm up there" roof inspection

You're already on the ladder. Looking at the roof line costs you nothing and adds value to the customer. A quick visual check for loose shingles, damaged flashing near the gutters, or debris buildup in valleys takes two minutes. Mentioning what you see ("I noticed a couple of shingles near the chimney look like they lifted, you might want to have a roofer take a look") builds trust, gets you remembered as thorough, and sometimes surfaces additional work.

This doesn't have to be a formal upsell. It's just: "While I was up there I noticed X, wanted to mention it." Customers remember the contractor who spotted a potential problem before it became a ceiling stain, and they call that contractor again next year without shopping around.

Gutter guard brands and separate LSA targeting

If you carry specific brand lines like LeafGuard, MasterShield, Gutter Helmet, or similar products, make sure "gutter guard installation" is enabled as a separate LSA job type. Homeowners who search "leaf guard gutters near me" or "gutter guard installation" are already sold on the concept. They just need someone to do it. That search is a warm lead for a ,500 to ,000 job, entirely separate from your cleaning and repair traffic. Don't miss it because the box was unchecked.

How to Rank Higher in Gutter LSA

Google's LSA algorithm weighs the same core factors across all trades. For the full deep-dive on every ranking signal, see our Google LSA ranking factors guide. For gutter companies specifically, here's how those factors play out in practice:

Reviews: quantity and recency both matter

Gutter companies with 40 or more reviews and a 4.7+ star average rank above competitors with fewer reviews in most markets. The challenge for gutters: customers don't always think to leave a review after a cleaning because it's a fast, low-drama service. Build review requests into your post-job process. Send a text within two hours of completing the job: "Hi [Name], thanks for having us out today. If we did a good job, a quick Google review makes a real difference for a small business. Here's the link: [URL]." Short, direct, and sent while the experience is still fresh. For a complete review collection system, see our LSA review strategy guide.

Response rate: answer the phone

Google tracks how quickly you respond to calls and messages, and factors your responsiveness directly into your ranking. Gutter contractors who answer 90%+ of calls within the first few rings rank higher than those who send callers to voicemail regularly. During fall storm season, this matters most. Set up call forwarding, use a virtual receptionist during peak hours, or stagger your crew schedules so someone is always available. A missed call during peak season is a double loss: you lose the job and you get a negative ranking signal.

All job types enabled

Every unchecked job type is a search category where you're invisible. A gutter contractor who only enables "gutter cleaning" and "gutter installation" misses every search for gutter guards, downspout repair, fascia repair, seamless gutters, and commercial gutter services. Each is a separate search query with its own demand. Enable everything you can deliver and review the list quarterly.

Before-and-after photos

Gutter work has a clear visual story. A photo of packed, overflowing gutters next to a clean, properly flowing system with new sections installed tells that story in two seconds. Upload 10 to 15 job photos minimum: cleaning jobs before and after, installation progress shots, gutter guard system results, and any commercial projects. Before-and-after photos are your strongest trust signal for a trade where customers can't verify quality until after the work is done.

Bid strategy: start broad, then optimize

Start with Maximize Leads bid mode for the first four to six weeks. Let Google's algorithm find your best leads before you introduce a Max Per Lead constraint. Once you have a baseline CPL, set your max per lead at 20 to 30% above your target CPL to stay competitive without overpaying. Common LSA Mistakes Gutter Companies Make

Not increasing budget before fall

The single most common and most expensive mistake. If your weekly budget cap is set to 00 and fall storms hit, you'll exhaust your budget by Tuesday and go dark for the rest of the week. That's 60% of your best leads going to competitors because you ran out of money. Check your weekly cap in late August and increase it to at least double your off-season level before September. You can always pull it back if volume is lower than expected. You cannot recover the leads you missed while you were paused.

Failing to enable gutter guard as a separate job type

Homeowners searching "gutter guard installation near me" are pre-sold on the concept and ready to buy. They're not cleaning customers looking for a cheap fix. They're installation customers looking for a permanent solution. If gutter guard installation isn't enabled as a job type, you're invisible for that search, and that search delivers one of the highest-ticket, lowest-friction leads in the gutter category.

Slow response on storm calls

Storm-damage callers are motivated and in decision-making mode. They're calling two or three contractors and booking the one who picks up first and can get out fastest. If you call back an hour later, that job is gone. During storm season, your phone needs to be answered. Use call forwarding, a scheduling app, or a virtual receptionist if you're on a job site. Every unanswered storm call is a real loss.

Not offering same-day or next-day service during peak windows

Gutter cleaning is a same-day service for most fall customers. A homeowner with overflowing gutters in October who hears "I can get there in a week and a half" will call the next number on the list immediately. Build your fall schedule to accommodate same-day and next-day slots. Customers who get fast service leave better reviews, refer more people, and call you again next year without shopping around.

No annual maintenance reminder system

Every cleaning customer you book from LSA is a potential recurring annual account. A simple text or email in early September and early April saying "Fall gutter cleaning season is here, want to get on the schedule?" turns a one-time 25 job into a recurring revenue stream that costs you nothing to acquire. Most gutter companies don't have this system, which means they're paying for LSA leads every season for customers they already own.

Service area set too large

More zip codes does not mean more profitable leads. Setting your service area to 40 zip codes means you're competing in markets that might take your crew 90 minutes to reach, spending budget on leads you can't serve profitably. Start with your core 10 to 15 zip codes where travel time is under 30 minutes and you can deliver same-day or next-day service during peak season. Expand once you have the crew capacity to handle it.

Should Gutter Companies Run LSA and Google Ads Together?

Yes, and the combination works well for gutters because the two platforms capture different buyer stages that don't overlap.

LSA captures the ready-to-call homeowner. "Gutter cleaning near me," "gutter repair," "gutter installation," "gutter guard installation near me." These searches come from people with an active need right now. They want someone to show up and solve the problem. LSA owns this moment because the listing puts you first, verified, with reviews, and a call button. There's no friction between intent and action.

Google Search Ads capture the research phase. Searches like "how much does gutter installation cost," "best gutter guards for pine needles," "K-style vs. half-round gutters," "seamless gutters vs. sectional," and "do gutter guards actually work" are research-mode searches. These buyers aren't ready to call today, but they'll be ready soon. Google Ads landing pages can capture their information and bring them back when they're ready to book. LSA doesn't touch this traffic at all.

The seasonal complement matters especially for gutters. During peak fall season, LSA may exhaust its budget before the week ends. Google Ads running in parallel can capture overflow demand for specific high-value queries like "seamless gutter installation" or "gutter guard system" when your LSA budget is spent for the week.

Where to start: Begin with LSA only. It's simpler to manage, the lead quality is strong, and it builds your review base faster than almost any other channel. Once you're consistently booking jobs and your crew has capacity for more, layer in Google Ads to capture the research-phase traffic and storm-season overflow. Frequently Asked Questions

Should I run LSA year-round or just during peak season?
Run it year-round, but reduce your budget by 40-50% during slow months (December-January, June-August in northern markets). Pausing completely hurts your ranking and resets your algorithm momentum. A lower budget in the off-season keeps your account active while controlling spend, and when fall demand returns, you're already ranked instead of rebuilding from zero. For more on budget planning by season, see our Google LSA cost guide.
What's the best way to handle emergency storm-damage calls from LSA?
Answer immediately and offer same-day or next-day service. Storm-damage leads are among the highest-converting calls in the home services industry because the homeowner is motivated and has a visible problem. Have a storm-response script ready: acknowledge the urgency, confirm your availability, and close on a time before ending the call. Every hour you wait, a competitor takes that job. For more on response time and its impact on conversion rates, see our LSA troubleshooting guide.
Is gutter cleaning or gutter installation more profitable on LSA?
Installation has the higher ticket (,200-,500), but cleaning has higher volume and faster close rates. The best businesses use cleaning jobs to build relationships and then upsell guard systems or full replacements when the gutters are too far gone to clean. Enable both and track them separately to understand which drives more revenue for your specific market. For overall lead volume benchmarks, see our LSA lead volume guide.
Do I need a license to do gutter work?
Requirements vary by state. Most states treat gutter installation as a home improvement project requiring a contractor license or registration. Cleaning is generally unlicensed but requires insurance. Google will verify your state's specific requirements during the LSA application. Check your state licensing board before starting. For a step-by-step walkthrough of the verification process, see our Google Verified guide.
How do I compete with larger gutter franchises like LeafFilter on LSA?
Franchises have deep pockets for advertising but they're selling a branded product at premium prices. You compete on reviews, local trust, and response time. A local gutter company with 60 verified 5-star reviews and same-day service consistently wins over a franchise with a 2 to 3 day wait time, even at a similar price point. Local credibility is your advantage, and LSA's verification badge puts you on equal footing right from the search results. For a full review-building system, see our LSA review strategy guide.
Should I offer a free inspection as a lead magnet for LSA calls?
Yes, for installation leads. Offering a free gutter inspection on large-project calls gets you on the property, which is your highest-leverage closing opportunity. For cleaning leads, just close on the call directly: you already know what you're going to do, and adding an inspection step creates unnecessary friction. Know the difference and handle each call type accordingly. Installation and gutter guard callers are worth the on-site visit. Cleaning callers want a price and a date.

Short answer (for featured snippets)

Most "LSA no calls" problems stem from one or more of these: incomplete verification, weak profile conversion signals (reviews/photos/description), missed or slow call handling, service-area or category mismatch, low local demand, or technical call-routing issues. Fix verification and responsiveness first, then improve reviews and targeting.

Quick diagnostic checklist (2 minutes)

Before you dig into the details, check these high-impact items:

  • Account status: Active / Verified (not Under Review or Suspended)
  • Impressions vs calls: are you getting impressions but no calls?
  • Recent reviews: any in the last 90 days? Overall rating above 4.5?
  • Service area: set correctly for your local market and not overly broad?
  • Call routing: forwarding numbers tested and working?
  • Lead disputes: did you recently decline or dispute many leads?

The 12 most common reasons (and how to fix each)

1) Verification or approval is incomplete

Diagnosis: Dashboard shows "Under review" or pending documents.
Fix: Upload exactly the documents requested (license, insurance, IDs). For a full walkthrough of the verification process, see our guide on how to get Google Guaranteed. Save screenshots of uploads with timestamps. If verification stalls for more than 7 days, open a support ticket and include the verification/task ID and your proof of submission.

2) You have impressions but zero calls — poor conversion

Diagnosis: Impressions > 0 but Calls = 0.
Fix: Improve trust and relevancy: get recent reviews, show job photos, tighten your primary service, and write a short, clear business description that matches common search phrases (e.g., "emergency plumber," "24/7 HVAC repair").

3) Low or stale reviews

Diagnosis: Few recent reviews or rating below ~4.5.
Fix: Implement a review system: one-click SMS link after jobs, a quick in-person script for techs, and automatic reminders. Respond publicly and professionally to negative reviews. Homeowners in your area heavily weigh review recency when choosing between two similar LSA profiles — a profile with 3 reviews in the last month consistently converts better than one with 40 reviews and nothing new in six months.

4) Lead quality problems (spam, duplicates, out-of-area)

Diagnosis: Many leads aren't actual jobs you want.
Fix: Tighten ZIP codes to your actual service area, limit listed services to what you perform well, add screening questions to your call script, and document invalid leads thoroughly before disputing in the dashboard.

5) Missed calls or slow response

Diagnosis: Missed calls, voicemail, or long callback delays.
Fix: Assign a dedicated LSA responder; use an answering service trained to qualify and schedule; set a 10–15 minute callback SLA and track answer-time KPI.

6) Budget vs demand mismatch

Diagnosis: Budget not spent or very low lead volume despite an active profile.
Fix: LSA delivery is demand-driven — confirm your categories and test neighboring ZIPs with proven demand from homeowners in your area before increasing budget. If local search volume is simply low for your trade category, expanding your service radius or running supplemental Google Search Ads may be necessary to maintain consistent lead flow.

7) Suspensions or policy flags

Diagnosis: Suspension notice or policy email.
Fix: Follow reinstatement instructions, gather requested documents, and escalate to support with the suspension code if the issue is unclear.

8) Overly broad or incorrect service area

Fix: Focus on dense ZIPs where you already have customers in your local market. Expand only after the profile shows stable performance in core areas. A service area that covers too many ZIP codes can dilute your relevancy score with Google — tighter is often better when starting out.

9) Competitors outrank you

Fix: Audit competitor profiles for review counts and response speeds. Improve review velocity, response time, and consider targeting narrower niche services to win targeted queries from customers searching near them. For a detailed breakdown of every factor Google uses to rank LSA profiles — and exactly what to fix — see our guide on LSA ranking factors.

10) Call routing or tracking is broken

Diagnosis: Test calls drop, forwarding fails, or no calls logged.
Fix: Test routing from multiple carriers/devices, validate call-tracking scripts, and keep a short manual log of test calls to confirm delivery.

11) Messaging leads are enabled but ignored

Fix: Forward message alerts to email/SMS or disable messaging leads if you won't monitor them. Customers searching near them expect a fast response regardless of channel — an unanswered message lead is treated the same as a missed call in Google's performance signals.

12) Seasonal or market-wide demand drops

Fix: Accept seasonality, expand temporarily into adjacent areas, diversify services, or run a short promotional offer to boost conversions during slow months.

Step-by-step troubleshooting flow (use this in order)

  1. Confirm account status (Active & Verified).
  2. If impressions = 0 → check verification, categories, and service area.
  3. If impressions > 0 but calls = 0 → fix conversion signals (reviews, photos, description).
  4. If calls are poor quality → tighten area, refine services, add screening, and document disputes.
  5. If calls are missed → fix routing and assign a dedicated responder.
  6. If everything looks fine but volume is low → test adjacent ZIPs and consider a short Search Ads pilot for demand data. For step-by-step guidance on running LSA and Google Ads together, see the Plumbing Ads Playbook.

Copy-paste scripts & templates

Review request SMS (one-click link)

Hi [First name], thanks for choosing [Company]. If we did a great job, could you leave a quick 1-minute review? [one-click review link] — it helps small businesses like ours. Thanks!

Call screening opener

Thanks for calling [Company]. This is [Name]. Is this an emergency or a routine appointment? Great — what's the address and the best callback number?

Dispute lead template (to Google support)

Account: [account ID]
Lead ID: [lead ID]
Date/time: [date]
Reason: wrong service / spam / duplicate
Attached: call log transcript / screenshot / technician note
Please review and refund/mark invalid.

KPIs to track weekly

  • Impressions → Calls → Bookings (conversion rates at each step)
  • Median call answer time (minutes)
  • Invalid leads vs disputes won
  • New reviews per 30/60/90 days
  • Lead cost (if tracking spend vs leads)

When to call LSA support

Contact support if verification is stuck for more than 7 days, if your account is suspended without a clear reason, or if you suspect a technical issue with call routing or duplicate leads. Before you call, have the following ready: your account ID, the specific task or suspension ID from your dashboard, screenshots of any documents you've already submitted, and a brief description of what changed before the issue started. Having this information on hand typically cuts resolution time in half.

Not every slow period requires a support call — many performance dips are self-correctable. If you've confirmed your account is Active and Verified, your reviews are recent, and your routing is working, a drop in volume is usually demand-driven or seasonal rather than a technical error. Support tickets are most effective when there's a clear account-level issue (a verification flag, a policy hold, or a billing error) rather than a general concern about lead volume in your local market. Keep a simple log of the date you opened the ticket, the issue description, and the representative's name — this speeds up follow-up considerably if the issue isn't resolved on the first contact.

Common mistakes that kill LSA performance

  • Assuming LSA is "set-and-forget" — it needs weekly attention.
  • Disputing too many leads without documentation (hurts dispute success rate).
  • Setting an overly broad service area that dilutes relevancy.
  • Passing calls to an untrained voicemail or third party that can't schedule jobs.

These mistakes tend to compound. A broad service area generates more out-of-area leads. You dispute more leads without clean documentation. Your dispute win rate drops. Google's algorithm interprets the pattern as low-quality matching and reduces your impressions further. The fix is almost always operational before it's technical — tighten your ZIP codes, train whoever answers LSA calls on basic job screening, and write a one-sentence dispute note for each lead before submitting. If your profile has been underperforming for more than 60 days and none of the above fixes have moved the needle, review whether your LSA ranking factors are limiting your visibility in local search results before assuming the problem is budget or demand. Quick action plan — do this today

  1. Confirm account is Active & Verified.
  2. Review last 30 days: impressions vs calls ratio.
  3. Push for 3 recent reviews (SMS + ask at completion).
  4. Test call routing with 5 manual calls and log results.
  5. Tighten service area by removing problem ZIPs outside your local market.
  6. Create a short LSA script and assign one person to handle LSA leads.

FAQ (short answers for snippets)

Are reviews the only ranking factor?

No — reviews are important, but Google also considers responsiveness, booking rate, proximity, and profile completeness.

Will raising my budget get more calls?

Not automatically. Budget helps only when there is sufficient qualified demand in your area and category. Prioritize relevance and conversion first.

How fast should I answer LSA calls?

Aim to answer live within 20 seconds and return missed leads within 10–15 minutes.

Google LSA for Painting Contractors: Get More Paint Jobs From Local Search in 2026

Here's something most painting contractors don't know: painting is one of the least digitally competitive home service categories on Google LSA. That's not because there aren't a lot of painters. There are plenty. The reason is that the vast majority of painters still run their entire business on referrals and word of mouth, and when they think about online advertising, they assume it's for HVAC companies and plumbers, not for them.

That assumption is a gift to the painter who figures it out first. While your local competitors are waiting on a cousin to pass along a phone number, you could be showing up at the top of Google the moment someone in your service area searches "interior painter near me" or "kitchen cabinet painting" and picking up a $2,500 job that was never going to come through a referral chain.

Painting is also uniquely well-suited to LSA for a reason most guides skip: homeowners letting a painter into their house for two to five days have a very different level of trust required compared to a plumber fixing a pipe in 45 minutes. The Google Guaranteed badge, background checks, and insurance verification all matter more in painting than in almost any other trade, and they're built right into the LSA listing. You get credibility by default that most painters in your market can't replicate with a Yelp profile or a Facebook page.

Is LSA Worth It for Painting Contractors?

Yes, with the right setup. Let's do the actual math because this is where LSA for painters gets interesting.

Interior painting generates leads year-round. Your estimating calendar doesn't have to go quiet in March just because it's not deck season. Exterior painting is seasonal, peaking April through October in most U.S. markets, but that's a long peak. Cabinet painting and refinishing? No seasonality at all. Homeowners remodel kitchens in January as readily as in July.

On the revenue side, painting jobs carry real value: interior residential projects run $1,200 to $4,500 on average depending on home size and scope. Exterior painting runs $2,500 to $8,000 or more for a full house. Kitchen cabinet refinishing typically lands at $1,500 to $4,500. These are not small tickets.

On the cost side, painting CPL (cost per lead) runs $25 to $55 in suburban markets and $45 to $85 in major metros, lower than plumbing or HVAC in most markets because fewer painters are advertising. At a $40 CPL with a reasonable answer rate and booking rate, you're paying under $100 to book a job worth $2,000 to $3,000. That math works extremely well.

What Google LSA Looks Like for Painters

When someone searches "interior painters near me," "cabinet painting contractor," or "exterior house painting estimate," Google surfaces a row of Local Services Ads at the very top of the page. We're talking above Google Ads, above the map pack, above every organic result. The people who get to your profile through LSA are not idly browsing Pinterest for inspiration. They're ready to call someone.

Your LSA listing shows your business name, star rating, review count, years in business, and a direct click-to-call button. On mobile, that call button is front and center. This is important because painting customers decide fast when they've finally committed to getting a room done.

The Google Guaranteed badge (displayed as "Google Verified" on many profiles) matters more in painting than it does in trades where a customer barely interacts with the crew. Painters are in someone's home for days. They're moving furniture, working around kids, touching everything. A homeowner choosing between three painters in the LSA pack is going to weight the verified, background-checked option heavily, especially if the alternative is an unverified listing with no reviews.

Photos also appear in your LSA profile, and painting is one of the few trades where photos in your listing directly change whether someone picks up the phone. More on that in the photos section below.

How to Set Up Google LSA for Your Painting Business

The setup process takes some patience upfront, mostly around document gathering. Once you're verified and live, the ongoing management is minimal. Here's the step-by-step:

  1. Create your profile at ads.google.com/local-services-ads. Add your business name, service areas, phone number, business hours, and a description. Keep the description concrete. "Professional residential painter serving the Denver metro area" beats "providing quality painting services for all your needs."
  2. Select your painting job types. Don't stop at interior and exterior. The full list of painting job types in LSA includes: interior painting, exterior painting, cabinet painting and refinishing, deck staining, fence painting and staining, wallpaper removal, commercial painting, drywall painting, and epoxy floor coating. Enable every category you can legitimately fulfill.
  3. Set your service area correctly. Painters can only reasonably travel so far for a three to five day interior job. Don't set a 50-mile radius if you're not willing to drive 45 minutes each way for a week. Over-extending your service area dilutes your ranking and produces leads you can't profitably serve.
  4. Upload your license and insurance documents. What Google requires varies by state (more on this below). Have digital copies of your general liability insurance and any required state or local contractor licenses ready to upload. Expired documents are the number one reason painters get stuck in verification.
  5. Complete background checks. Owners need a background check. In some markets, technicians who work in customers' homes do too. This step is handled through a Google third-party partner and can take a week or more. Start it before you've finished everything else.
  6. Connect your Google Business Profile. Your GBP reviews are what populate your LSA listing's star rating. If your GBP has 40 reviews averaging 4.8 stars, that transfers directly into your LSA ranking power. Make sure your GBP is accurate, complete, and actively collecting reviews before you launch.
  7. Add photos to your profile. Real before-and-after photos of jobs you've completed. Not stock images. This is non-negotiable for painters. See the photos section below for exactly what to shoot.
  8. Set your budget and bidding mode. Start with Maximize Leads and a weekly budget calibrated for 10 to 15 leads per week at your expected CPL. You can adjust once you have a few weeks of data.

Painter Licensing and Google LSA Verification

This section matters a lot for painters specifically because the licensing situation for painting contractors is genuinely confusing, and it trips people up during Google's verification process more than in other trades.

Unlike HVAC, electrical, or plumbing, where most states require a specific trade license, painter licensing requirements vary wildly by state:

  • Some states have no license requirement for residential painting under a certain dollar threshold. In those states, Google will still require general liability insurance and background checks, but you don't upload a trade license.
  • Some states require a general contractor license for any painting job over a certain value. If that's your state and you don't have one, you may need to get licensed before you can complete LSA verification.
  • A handful of states have specific painting contractor licenses. California, for example, has a C-33 painting and decorating contractor license. Louisiana requires a specific painting license. If your state issues one, Google will want it.
  • Local jurisdictions sometimes have additional requirements. Some cities and counties layer requirements on top of state law. Know both before you apply.

The fix is simple: before you start the LSA application, look up your state's contractor licensing requirements for painting specifically (search "[your state] painting contractor license requirements"). Know what applies to you, get the documents together, and upload them immediately when prompted. Painters who stall in verification almost always got stuck because they uploaded the wrong document or waited to dig up their insurance certificate.

If your state doesn't require a license, don't leave the license field empty. Include a note explaining that your state has no licensing requirement for painting contractors. This reduces the chance of getting a manual review request asking for clarification.

What Does LSA Cost for Painting Contractors?

Painting falls into a cost range that makes the economics comfortable compared to higher-CPL categories like water damage or roofing.

  • Suburban markets: $25 to $55 per lead for residential painting
  • Major metro markets: $45 to $85 per lead (more competition, higher property values, but also higher average job values)
  • Cabinet painting: Often at the lower end of the range because fewer painters have this enabled, meaning less competition per search
  • Commercial painting: CPL can run higher but job values are proportionally much larger

Here's what the cost-per-booked-job math looks like for a typical painting business:

  • Cost per lead: $40
  • Answer rate (percentage of calls you pick up): 75%
  • Booking rate (percentage of answered calls that schedule an estimate): 60%
  • Cost per booked estimate: $40 / (0.75 x 0.60) = $89
  • Average job value: $2,000
  • ROAS (return on ad spend): roughly 22x

Even if your close rate on estimates is 50% (meaning only half of estimates turn into signed jobs), your cost per booked job is $178 against a $2,000 average, which is still an excellent return. For exterior paint jobs with an average value of $4,000 to $5,000, the economics get even better.

Seasonality and LSA for Painters

Your estimating calendar is full from May through September, and then things go quiet. If that pattern sounds familiar, LSA doesn't change the underlying seasonality of exterior painting, but it does give you tools to smooth it out.

Here's how the different painting categories break down by season:

Painting Type CPL Range Avg Job Value Seasonality Competition Level
Interior Painting $25–$55 $1,200–$4,500 Year-round Moderate
Exterior Painting $35–$65 $2,500–$8,000+ Apr–Oct peak High (peak season)
Cabinet Painting $25–$45 $1,500–$4,500 Year-round Lower (underserved)
Deck Staining $30–$55 $800–$2,500 Spring peak Moderate
Commercial Painting $50–$90 $5,000–$30,000+ Year-round Lower (specialized)

CPL ranges reflect typical U.S. suburban markets. Metro markets may run 20–40% higher.

The strategic play here is to shift your emphasis rather than your budget. During October through February, make sure interior painting, cabinet painting, drywall painting, and wallpaper removal are prominently featured in your profile. These categories don't care about the weather. A homeowner repainting a living room in January is a perfectly normal lead.

Deck staining peaks in spring because homeowners are assessing their outdoor spaces after winter. Get your deck staining job type enabled and your budget ready before March, not after.

The painters who kill it with LSA are the ones who see winter not as dead season but as cabinet season and interior season. They keep the phone ringing at a reduced but steady pace, maintain their review momentum, and show up at the top of the LSA pack when exterior season hits in April with a profile that's been staying active all winter.

Photos and Portfolio in Your Painter LSA Profile

If you take nothing else from this guide, take this: photos matter more for painters on LSA than for almost any other trade category.

Think about it from the homeowner's perspective. When they call an electrician, they can't really preview the finished work. The panel is in the basement. The outlets look the same as before. But when they hire a painter, the entire visual output of months of living in their home is in your hands. They're choosing a color transformation. They want to see that you can deliver it.

Painters with 10 or more high-quality before-and-after photos in their LSA profile consistently outperform painters with no photos or generic images. Here's what photographs well and what to prioritize:

  • Exterior color transformations. A house going from faded tan to a rich navy or clean white with fresh trim is visually striking. Show the before, show the after. These are scroll-stopping images.
  • Kitchen cabinet refinishing. Before and after cabinet work is extremely persuasive because the transformation is obvious and everyone knows what beat-up cabinets look like. If you do cabinet work, this should be in your profile.
  • Clean trim and cut-in lines. Professionals who show a crisp window frame or a perfectly cut-in ceiling line are demonstrating craftsmanship in a single photo. Homeowners who've seen messy paint lines from a bad painter will respond strongly to this.
  • Interior room transformations. A living room going from dated beige to a warm accent wall with neutral surround tells a story quickly. Include both before and after shots.
  • Your crew at work. Photos of your team in branded shirts with professional equipment (not rollers from the dollar store) build confidence in your professionalism and reinforce that you're insured and legitimate.

Take photos on every job. Shoot with your phone in good natural light, ideally at the start of the day before you've covered anything. Clean up the foreground so the photo shows the space at its best. You don't need a professional photographer, just consistency. Make it part of your job close-out process.

How to Rank Higher in Painting LSA

Google's LSA ranking algorithm weighs several signals, and painting has some unique considerations that most generic guides don't address.

  • Review volume and recency are the top factors. Aim for a steady cadence: four to eight reviews per month beats 30 reviews in October followed by silence until spring. Send review requests after every completed job, every time. A simple text with a direct link to your Google Business Profile takes 30 seconds and is the highest-leverage action you can take for your LSA ranking. For a complete system, see our LSA review strategy guide.
  • Review content matters for painters specifically. Reviews that mention specific details like color matches, clean trim lines, careful furniture moving, and punctuality signal quality to Google's algorithm and to future customers reading your profile. When you request reviews, ask customers to describe what they had painted and how the result turned out. Specific beats generic every time.
  • Answer your phone. Google tracks your call response rate and factors it into ranking. A missed call from an LSA lead counts against you. If you're on a job site, set up call forwarding or use a virtual receptionist during peak hours. A painter who answers immediately and schedules an estimate on the first call has a massive advantage over one who calls back three hours later.
  • Enable every applicable job type. Each job type you enable opens a category of search queries that you were previously invisible for. Cabinet painting alone represents thousands of monthly searches in mid-size markets. Don't leave that on the table.
  • Keep your service area realistic. Google factors proximity into ranking. A tighter, more accurate service area means you rank better within it rather than spreading thin across a huge radius where you can't actually compete on response time and scheduling.
  • Complete profile beats incomplete profile, always. Business hours, photos, license documents, a complete business description, connected GBP. Every blank field is a missed ranking signal.

Common Mistakes Painting Companies Make With LSA

  • Not enabling cabinet painting. This is the single most common missed opportunity for painters on LSA. Cabinet refinishing is a booming category with strong year-round demand, high job values, and lower competition than interior or exterior painting because so few painters have bothered to enable it. If you do cabinet work, turn this on immediately.
  • Leaving deck staining and fence painting off. These are separate job type categories in LSA with their own search volumes. A homeowner searching "deck staining near me" won't find you if you only have interior and exterior painting enabled. Check the full list of available categories for your trade and turn on everything you offer.
  • Setting a service area that's too large. A painter can only do so many jobs in a week, and driving 45 minutes to a job site for a three-day project burns time that could have been spent on a job around the corner. Oversized service areas lower your ranking within your actual operating radius. Set the area you can realistically and profitably serve.
  • No photos in the profile. Painting is a visual trade. A profile with zero photos in a market where a competitor has 15 before-and-after shots is going to lose the conversion even if both listings have similar reviews. Add photos before you go live, and keep adding them.
  • Not responding to negative reviews. Painting is a service where trust is everything, and your LSA profile is highly visible. A negative review with no response is worse than a negative review where you responded professionally, acknowledged the concern, and explained how you made it right. Homeowners reading your profile are watching how you handle conflict, not just counting stars.
  • Turning off LSA entirely in winter. Your ranking doesn't pause gracefully and restart at full strength. It decays. Competitors who stayed active through November and December are ahead of you in March when exterior season starts. Keep a reduced budget running year-round, shift the emphasis to interior and cabinet work, and keep collecting reviews. You're building a position, not just buying leads.

Should Painters Run LSA and Google Ads Together?

Start with LSA. Get verified, get your profile dialed in, collect reviews, and build your ranking before you layer in anything else. LSA is the right first channel for most painting contractors because the intent is high (people searching for a painter are ready to get an estimate), the setup is manageable, and you only pay for actual leads.

Google Search Ads make sense as a complement once you're ready to grow beyond what LSA alone can deliver, or when you want to pursue categories that LSA doesn't cover well. Commercial painting contracts, for example, are often researched by property managers who aren't using the same urgent near-me search patterns that LSA captures best. A Google Ads campaign targeting commercial painting keywords with a dedicated landing page can reach that audience in a way LSA can't.

Google Ads also lets you run promotions, like a spring exterior painting special or a cabinet refinishing offer in January, with dedicated ad copy and landing pages. LSA doesn't support promotional messaging in the listing format.

FAQ

Do painting contractors need a license for Google LSA?

It depends on your state. Licensing requirements for painters vary more than almost any other trade. Some states require no license at all for residential painting under a certain dollar threshold. Others require a general contractor license. A handful issue specific painting contractor licenses. Google checks what your state mandates and requires proof during verification. Research your state's requirements before you start the LSA application so you have the right documents ready. If your state has no licensing requirement, note that clearly when uploading documents rather than leaving the field blank.

What are the best job types to enable for painters on LSA?

Enable everything you can legitimately deliver. At minimum: interior painting, exterior painting, cabinet painting and refinishing, deck staining, fence painting and staining, wallpaper removal, and commercial painting. Most painters only turn on interior and exterior and miss cabinet refinishing, which is a fast-growing category where kitchen cabinet jobs alone average $1,500 to $4,500. Also enable drywall painting and epoxy floor coating if you offer those. Every unchecked job type is a category of searches you're completely invisible for.

How much does LSA cost for painting contractors?

Painting CPL runs $25 to $55 in most suburban markets and $45 to $85 in major metros. Interior painting tends to be at the lower end of that range. Cabinet painting often has lower competition, which means a lower CPL despite strong job values. At $40 CPL with a 75% answer rate and 60% booking rate, you're paying roughly $89 per booked estimate. Against an average job value of $2,000 or more, that's a strong return on ad spend that most painting contractors aren't taking advantage of yet.

Is LSA better for interior or exterior painting?

Both work well, but they have different seasonal profiles. Interior painting generates year-round leads because homeowners repaint rooms in any season. Exterior painting peaks April through October in most markets because you can't properly paint in cold temperatures. The smart approach is to keep LSA running year-round and shift your profile's job type emphasis toward interior, cabinet, and drywall painting in fall and winter rather than pausing your entire budget. Exterior leads during prime season are typically high-value, so the increased competition is worth running through.

How important are photos for a painter's LSA profile?

Very important, more so than for most other trades. Painting is entirely visual, and homeowners choosing between painters in the LSA pack want to see finished work before they call. Painters with 10 or more quality before-and-after photos consistently see better conversion rates than those with no photos. Exterior color transformations, kitchen cabinet refinishing, and clean trim lines photograph especially well. Make shooting before-and-after photos part of your standard job close-out process. Real photos of real work build the trust that turns a lead into a booked estimate.

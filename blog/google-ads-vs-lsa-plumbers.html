What Are Local Services Ads (LSA)?

Local Services Ads appear at the very top of Google search results, above traditional ads and organic listings. These ads show your business with reviews, location, and the Google Verified badge — telling customers searching near them that your business has been screened and approved by Google.

Instead of paying per click, you pay per lead — when someone calls or messages your business directly through the ad.

For plumbers, this is powerful because most users who click LSA ads are already looking to hire someone immediately. They're not browsing local search results to learn about plumbing — they have a burst pipe or a drain that won't clear and they need help today.

How Google Ads Works for Plumbers

Google Ads (Search Ads) show up below Local Services Ads and allow you to bid on keywords like "emergency plumber near me" or "drain cleaning service."

With Google Ads, you pay for every click — whether the person becomes a customer or not. This gives you more control, but also more risk if campaigns aren't managed properly.

When structured correctly, Google Ads can generate consistent volume and scale faster than LSA alone — capturing homeowners in your area who are researching water heater replacement costs, comparing drain cleaning services, or looking for a plumber for a scheduled job.

Lead Quality Comparison

In most plumbing markets, Local Services Ads tend to produce higher-quality leads. Customers trust the Google Verified badge and are usually ready to book. They've already searched in their local area and made a decision to reach out — you're not competing against a page of results, you're the first call they make.

Google Ads can generate strong leads too, but they often include:

  • Price shoppers
  • Research-only visitors
  • Comparison shoppers
  • Out-of-area clicks

Without strong filtering and negatives, Google Ads can attract low-intent traffic.

Cost Comparison

Local Services Ads typically cost between $25–$80 per lead for plumbers, depending on location and competition in your market.

Google Ads costs vary widely. In competitive markets, plumbers often pay $15–$40 per click. A few bad clicks can quickly drain a budget.

While Google Ads can produce lower cost-per-lead in some markets, it requires ongoing optimization. Understanding the factors that drive LSA performance alongside Google Ads management is what separates plumbers who grow their cost efficiency over time from those who keep overspending.

Setup and Management Difficulty

Local Services Ads require background checks, insurance verification, and profile approval. Once approved, setup is fairly simple.

Google Ads requires more technical skill:

  • Keyword research
  • Ad copywriting
  • Landing page optimization
  • Conversion tracking
  • Bid management

Without experience, most plumbing companies struggle to make Google Ads profitable.

Which Platform Is Better for Most Plumbers?

For most plumbing businesses, the best approach is:

  • Start with Local Services Ads for stable, high-intent leads from customers searching near you
  • Add Google Ads once LSA is performing consistently
  • Use both together for maximum market coverage

LSA provides reliability. Google Ads provides scalability. When combined, they create a strong lead generation system that covers both the urgent calls from local customers who need help now and the planned jobs from homeowners earlier in the decision process.

Common Mistakes Plumbers Make

  • Running Google Ads without call tracking
  • Leaving LSA profiles unoptimized
  • Ignoring lead disputes
  • Overbidding in competitive zones
  • Not answering calls fast enough

These mistakes compound on each other. An LSA profile with stale reviews and no photos will convert poorly no matter how much you spend on it. Add in missed calls and you're paying for a channel you've kneecapped before it can work. The fix for most of these is operational, not technical — answer faster, request reviews after every job, and log outcomes in your LSA dashboard so Google can learn what a good lead looks like for your business.

Advertising only works when paired with strong follow-up systems. If your team can't consistently answer calls during business hours and return missed calls within 15 minutes, fix that first — both LSA and Google Ads will perform significantly better with a reliable call handling process in place.

Final Recommendation

If you're choosing between Google Ads and Local Services Ads, start with LSA.

The dividing line is simple: LSA is lower risk, easier to manage, and the lead quality for urgent plumbing searches is consistently higher. You pay per contact, not per click. Once you have a call handling process in place and you're booking jobs consistently from LSA, add Google Ads to extend your reach into broader intent searches and scale your overall lead volume.

The plumbers who win long-term are the ones who build systems — not just campaigns. A well-optimized LSA profile combined with a tightly structured Google Ads account gives you coverage across every stage of the customer journey: from the homeowner who needs an emergency plumber right now to the one comparing options for a bathroom remodel three months out.

Results vary based on market competition, budget, seasonality, and follow-up processes.
